Lagos, Nigeria, April 16, 2024: Leading financial institution, Fidelity Bank Plc, has released its 2023 full year Audited Financial Statements, reporting a 131.5% growth in Profit Before Tax to N 124,26 billion.

According to the results, which was issued to the Nigerian Exchange (NGX) today, the bank grew Gross Earnings by 64.9% YoY to N555.83 billion, driven by 81.6% growth in Net interest income which increased from N152.7billion to N277.37 billion. This led to a Profit After Tax of N99.45 billion representing a 112.9% annual growth.

Commenting on the Bank’s commendable performance, Dr. Nneka Onyeali-Ikpe,OON, MD/CEO of Fidelity Bank Plc said, “We closed the financial year with strong double-digit growth across key income and balance-sheet lines. Our performance in 2023 is an attestation of our capacity to deliver superior returns to shareholders despite the difficulties in our operating environment. Profit before tax grew by 131.5% to N124.3bn from N53.7bn in 2022FY, leading to an increase in Return on Average Equity (RoAE) of 26.5% from 15.6% in 2022FY.”

A review of the financial performance showed that the bank grew Net interest income by 81.6% to N277.4bn driven by a 55.5% increase in interest income, thus reflecting a steady rise in asset yield throughout the year. The average funding cost dropped by 20bps to 4.4% due to increased low-cost funds that grew from 83.6% in 2022FY to 97.4% in 2023. The combination of higher asset yield and lower funding cost led to an increase in Net Interest Margin (NIM) of 8.1% from 6.3% in 2022FY.

Similarly, Total Customer Deposits crossed the N4tn mark as deposits grew by 55.6% from N2.6tn in 2022FY. The increase was driven by 81.1% growth in low-cost funds.

Despite the challenging operating environment, the bank reaffirmed its devotion to helping individuals grow, inspiring businesses to thrive and empowering economies to prosper by increasing Net Loans & Advances to N3.1tn from N2.1tn in 2022FY.

Despite the growth in its loan portfolio, Regulatory Ratios were maintained well above the required thresholds, with liquidity ratio at 45.3% from 39.6% in 2022FY and capital adequacy ratio (CAR) at 16.2% compared to the minimum requirement of 15.0%.

“We recognize the changing dynamics in the Nigerian banking space and the need to monitor and proactively manage evolving risks. The proposed final dividend of 60 kobo per share reflects our commitment to strong value creation and returns to our shareholders,” explained Onyeali-Ikpe.

Fidelity Bank has consistently paid dividend since 2006. With the proposed final dividend of 60 kobo per share, Fidelity Bank would be paying investors a total dividend of 85 kobo per share for the reporting period, a 70.0% increase compared to the 50 kobo per share paid to its shareholders in the previous year.

Ranked as one of the best banks in Nigeria, Fidelity Bank is a full-fledged customer commercial bank with over 8.3 million customers serviced across its 251 business offices in Nigeria and the United Kingdom as well as on digital banking channels.

The bank has won multiple local and international awards including the Export Finance Bank of the Year at the 2023 BusinessDay Banks and Other Financial Institutions (BAFI) Awards, the Best Payment Solution Provider Nigeria 2023 and Best SME Bank Nigeria 2022 by the Global Banking and Finance Awards; Best Bank for SMEs in Nigeria by the Euromoney Awards for Excellence 2023; and Best Domestic Private Bank in Nigeria by the Euromoney Global Private Banking Awards 2023.

]]> Lagos, the economic heartbeat of Nigeria, pulsates with opportunities for investors seeking vibrant markets and dynamic growth prospects. As Africa’s largest city by population and one of its fastest-growing economies, Lagos offers a compelling and profitable environment for investment across various sectors. 

10  crucial insights to consider when exploring investment opportunities in this bustling metropolis

1. Sustained robust Economic Growth: 

Lagos boasts a robust economy, contributing significantly to Nigeria’s GDP. With diverse sectors such as finance, technology, manufacturing, and entertainment driving its growth, the city offers a fertile ground for investors seeking high returns on their investments.

2. Strategic Location for Investment: 

Situated along the Atlantic Ocean coast, Lagos serves as a gateway to regional and international markets. Its strategic location facilitates trade and commerce, making it an attractive destination for businesses looking to expand their reach across West Africa and beyond.

3. Constant Booming Population: 

With a population exceeding 20 million people, Lagos presents a vast consumer market hungry for goods and services. The city’s burgeoning middle class, coupled with a youthful demographic, creates a dynamic consumer base ripe for investment in retail, real estate, and consumer goods.

4. Permanent Infrastructure Development: 

Despite facing infrastructural challenges, Lagos is undergoing significant development initiatives aimed at enhancing its transportation networks, power supply, and urban amenities. Investments in infrastructure projects present lucrative opportunities for investors looking to contribute to the city’s growth and development. For example, the fourth Mainland bridge, and solid road network in Lekki-Epe and other places.

5. Tech Innovation Hub: 

Lagos has emerged as a leading technology innovation hub in Africa, often referred to as “Africa’s Silicon Valley.” The city’s thriving tech ecosystem attracts entrepreneurs, startups, and venture capitalists, fostering innovation and entrepreneurship across various sectors, including fintech, e-commerce, and logistics.

6. Dynamic Real Estate Market: 

The demand for residential, commercial, and industrial real estate in Lagos continues to soar, driven by population growth and urbanization. Investors can capitalize on this demand by investing in property development, rental housing, and commercial spaces in prime locations such as AJAH, Lekki-Epe, EPE, and many more across the city.

7. Predictable Business-Friendly Environment: 

Lagos offers a conducive business environment characterized by relatively low tax rates, streamlined regulatory processes, and a growing culture of entrepreneurship. Investors benefit from government incentives and policies aimed at attracting foreign direct investment and promoting business growth.

8. Cultural Diversity and Vibrancy: 

As a melting pot of cultures and ethnicities, Lagos pulsates with energy and creativity. Its rich cultural heritage, vibrant arts scene, and dynamic nightlife contribute to its allure as a destination for leisure and entertainment investments, including hospitality, tourism, and events management.

9. Resilience and Adaptability: 

Despite facing occasional challenges such as infrastructure deficits, security concerns, and bureaucratic bottlenecks, Lagos has demonstrated resilience and adaptability in navigating these obstacles. Investors can leverage the city’s entrepreneurial spirit and ingenuity to mitigate risks and capitalize on emerging opportunities.

10. Sustainable Development Goals: 

Lagos is committed to achieving sustainable development goals, including environmental sustainability, social inclusion, and economic prosperity. Investors aligned with these goals can explore opportunities in renewable energy, waste management, healthcare, and education to contribute to the city’s sustainable growth trajectory.

In conclusion, a place like Lagos, Nigeria offers various investment opportunities that will be hugely profitable to you in the long run.
