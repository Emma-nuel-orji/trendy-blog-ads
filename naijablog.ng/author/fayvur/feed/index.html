Osun State government has received 15 trafficked girls from Lebanon. They were received by Mrs Olubukola Olaboopo, Commissioner For Women, Children and Social Affairs and the Senior Special Assistant to the Governor on Diaspora Matters, Mrs Titi Fadahunsi-Ajayi.

]]> Buhari inaugurates committee for 2050 Agenda

A committee for Nigeria’s new development plan, Agenda 2050 has been set up by President Muhammadu Buhari.

He appointed businessman, Atedo Peterside and finance minister, Zainab Ahmed as co-chairpersons of the committee.

Femi Adesina, the spokesperson of the President disclosed this information. The committee was inaugurated on Wednesday.

President Muhammadu Buhari Wednesday in Abuja inaugurated the National Steering Committee to oversee the development of the Nigeria Agenda 2050 and Medium-Term National Development Plan (MTNDP) to succeed Vision 20:2020 and the Economic Recovery and Growth Plan (ERGP) 2017 – 2020.

The National Steering Committee for the development of Successor Plans to Vision 20:2020 and ERGP will be jointly chaired by Atedo Peterside and Zainab Ahmed, the Minister of Finance, Budget and National Planning.”

]]> UN urges Nigeria to adhere to COVID-19 safety precautions

In line with school reopening in Nigeria, the United Nations has called on state authorities in the country to ensure that safety protocols against the spread of COVID-19 are in place before the full reopening of schools.

The agency said that about 46 million primary and secondary learners across Nigeria are affected due to pandemic-related school closures.

Edward Kallon, the UN Resident and Humanitarian Coordinator, made this known in a statement in commemoration of the World International Day to Protect Education from Attack.

The theme for the Day this year is: “Protect Education, Save a Generation.”

Mr Kallon said that prioritising safety in schools for educators and learners would be an indication of the government’s commitment to protecting investments in the education sector and a validation of Nigeria’s endorsement of the Safe Schools Declaration.

He said,

“As State Governments plan to reopen schools after prolonged closures, building a resilient education system to withstand future shocks should be included in pandemic response plans.

Education is essential to helping crisis-affected communities in the north-east rebuild and recover. Attacks on schools are a direct attack on future generations.

I call on all parties to the conflict to take all necessary measures to protect education and give learners a chance to build a brighter future.”
