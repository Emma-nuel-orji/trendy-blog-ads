]]> You really won’t have to wait too long to finish your project or deliver jobs to your clients again thanks to the best laptops for video editing. The question is, which laptop models are the greatest for your video editing needs, given the wide range of options available?

However, we have compiled a list of options that includes laptops with strong processors, sufficient RAM, and high-quality screens. The finest laptops for video editing must have these qualities, but you should be aware that they are not inexpensive if you want the high performance you require.

Making use of high-resolution video requires a lot of memory and processing power, so ensure sure your computer is capable of handling it. Even though it can be enticing to choose one of the best budget laptops. stead, you’ll probably find that this just won’t be able to adequately suit your needs – and you’ll just find yourself wishing you’d chosen one of these superior models anyway.

The best laptops for video editing are typically larger than average due to their enormous, gorgeous screens and ample memory. This shouldn’t be a problem for you if your work is often done in one location. But if you’re always on the run, you might want to choose a smaller laptop.

How To Pick The Best Laptops For Video Editing

What characteristics distinguish a good laptop for video editing? You must first obtain long-lasting battery power. Video editing can be demanding, especially when working with ultra-high-resolution footage, so you’ll need a laptop with enough RAM (at least 8GB, though 16GB is advisable).

You’ll also need a modern processor, such as one from Intel or AMD, or, if you’re looking for a MacBook, Apple’s M1 chip, which has proven to be an excellent piece of hardware for video editing.

Because video files can be quite large, video editors will also require a laptop with plenty of fast SSD storage. Even if you use external storage, you’ll want something that allows you to quickly open files or move them between the laptop and a backup drive, a perfect example is the following list of laptops.

1. Dell XPS 15

The Dell XPS 15 features an SD card port, up to an Intel Core i9 processor, a solid 64GB of RAM, and up to 2TB of solid-state storage, making it the perfect device for consuming gigabytes of 4K entertainment. Although its Nvidia GeForce RTX 3050 Ti graphics card won’t match up to some of the top-tier competition, you can replace it with an external, desktop GPU thanks to Thunderbolt 4 connectivity for more demanding projects, and for fast edits on the fly, it’ll be more than capable.

2. Acer ConceptD 7

The Acer Concept range is a collection of laptops and desktops that have been tailored for designers and editors if you have a lot of money to spend. The ConceptD 7-series laptop, in particular, boasts gaming laptop power without any garnish casing or eye-catching RGB lighting and equally pass for one of the best laptops for video editing. Additionally, it’s silent, keeping cool without having noisy fans whirling or roaring.

The Acer ConceptD 7’s 4K UHD display, which is excellent for color grading and completely covers the Adobe RGB gamut, is a remarkable feat that few other monitor screens can equal. You can depend on the display for work that requires color accuracy because it is Pantone validated and has a Delta E 2 color accuracy.

Featuring the most recent Intel hexa-core i7 internals, an Nvidia GeForce RTX 2080 graphics card, and higher. The recent Intel hexa-core i7 internals, an Nvidia GeForce RTX 2080 graphics card, and up to 32GB RAM should make quick work of processing Full HD or 4K footage on Acer’s ConceptD.

Mac Book Pro M1 (16-inches)

Our best pick for video editing is the slightly older but still fantastic 16-inch MacBook Pro until we’ve had the newest 2022 13-inch MacBook Pro with its M2 technology in for review. Despite having this much power, the M1 chip architecture’s exceptional efficiency and a huge 100-watt-hour battery allow for a remarkable 21 hours of battery life.

If you equip a device with Apple’s M1 Max CPU, which comes with up to 64GB of RAM, it will easily handle whatever you can throw at it. Additionally, you get a screen size of 16 inches, which is notably more pleasant for extended editing sessions than a panel of 13 inches. This amazing Liquid Retina XDR display can display a high DCI-P3 color space coverage for precise video editing and sports an unbelievable 1,600-nit peak brightness, bringing HDR content to life.

No matter how you configure one, the 2021 16-inch MacBook Pro is not cheap, but you are spending your money on one of the most cutting-edge laptops available today. A less expensive option is the MacBook Pro 14-inch (2021).

4. Asus ROG Zephyrus G15

Want a capable laptop for editing videos but don’t want to go the MacBook route? If you want a laptop computer with unrelenting computing power, this is it, in our opinion. There won’t be anything this beast can’t handle with its powerful octa-core AMD Ryzen 7 or Ryzen 9 processors and staggering 48GB of RAM.

You also have an option of Nvidia’s super-powerful GeForce RTX 30-series graphics cards, which are specifically made with gaming in mind. The additional video acceleration is not only excellent for gaming but also very useful for GPU-accelerated video encoding.

A 15.6-inch WQHD (2560 x 1440) IPS screen completes the package. Even though it isn’t in 4K, it nonetheless boasts 100 percent coverage of the difficult DCI-P3 video color space. A 15.6-inch WQHD (2560 x 1440) IPS screen completes the package. Okay, so it’s not in 4K, but it does support Dolby Vision HDR and has a whoppingly outstanding 100% coverage of the difficult DCI-P3 video color space.

It is unfortunate that there is just a microSD memory card slot available, and that the USB ports are USB 3.2 Gen 2 rather than the newest USB 4.0. However, these are only minor gripes with a machine that can edit videos extremely effectively.

5. Envy 17

The Envy 17 comes in a variety of spec configurations, but we believe the ’17-ch0020na’ version is best for serious video editing and is reasonably priced given the amount of power it provides. The HP Envy 17 with its larger than average 17-inch screen gives you appreciably more display real estate for video previews and timelines, as well as multitasking.

The screen itself is not, by any means, just quantity above quality. For a laptop display, the IPS LCD panel’s 4K (3840 x 2160) resolution, 400-nit maximum brightness, and ability to display the entirety of the Adobe RGB color space are impressive accomplishments. A full-size keyboard can fit in front of the larger screen, and you even have an actual SD card reader built in, which is extremely uncommon in modern laptops.

Conclusion

Welcome to the end of the review. Remember that before selecting the best laptops for video editing, take time to select a PC with broad screen, battery life, storage capacity and processing speed. This would help any novice and professional video editor to deliver the best visual content possible.
