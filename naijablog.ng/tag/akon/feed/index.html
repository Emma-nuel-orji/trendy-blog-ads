]]> The Senegalis born American singer, popular known as Akon has say he doesn’t do drugs nor smoke.

In circular that has gone viral on social media, Akon didn’t only says he doesn’t involved in drugs and smoking, but also praise himself as the only rapper in the whole music business that doesn’t smoke, drink, or do drugs.

Part of his words read:

“I get dizzy when someone smokes near me, like I just hate it. I once tried to wrap a blunt, but my dad snéâked behind me and whîppéd me so if I’m not smôkîng today, it’s all thanks to my dad, I decided to never smôké. Growing up, I was never pressed by péer pressure. You couldn’t make me do what I knew wasn’t right. It couldn’t just happen because I always sort to uphold all the morals my dad thought me.” Akon

Truth be told, a lot of people do funny things which they know are wrong just because they can’t resist péer pressure. Friends will tell you things like, “Grow up, one time won’t hurt,” and you’ll go ahead to do it knowing fully well that it isn’t right.

Many people have gone astray because they’re not living but existing and letting people decide their lives for them. In all you do, live a moral life because you aren’t here to impress people for everything you do. You shall bear the consequences alone

The post I Was Happier When I Was Poor — Singer Akon appeared first on Naijablog.

]]> 48-year-old Senegalese-American singer and businessman, Akon, has said he was much happier when he was poor because life is tougher for the rich than the poor.

He said this on Monday, October 4, while defending a statement he made in the wake of actor Michael K. Williams’ dath in September 2021. The artist had used Williams’ dath as proof that the more money some makes, the more problems they accumulate.

Speaking in an interview with TMZ, he said, “Nobody can sit there and tell me, that I didn’t go through poverty. I was the young African kid, barefoot and playing soccer in the village with no electricity, no running water, I knew what that looked like – believe me, I know what poverty looks like.

But I also know what success looks like, and from my personal experience, I’m having more problems successfully dealing with all that comes with success than I had when I was poor. I was actually happier when I was poor.”
