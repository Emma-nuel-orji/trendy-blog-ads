]]> No fewer than six police officers has killed in Akwa Ibom State as a Gunmen attack police station.

Gunmen believed to be members of the Indigenous People of Biafra (IPOB), launched the attack on Saturday Morning 8th of May 2021 on Odoro Ikpe Police Station, in Ini LGA of Akwa Ibom State.

Omokoshaban gathered that six Policemen on duty were Killed during the attack.

It was gathered that the attack occurred in the early hours of Saturday when people were still sleeping.

The attack is coming few hours after the same gunman attacked police stations in Rivers State killing seven police officers.

Recall that there has been recent attack on police stations in South East by unknown gunmen believed to be the indigenous people of Biafra IPOB.

This continue attack has since she was into the minds of some Nigerian believe that Nigeria will soon disintegrate.

But president muhammadu buhari has assured Nigerians that all the necessary measures have been put in place to stop the attack on police personnels and innocent Nigerians.

]]> Social media users are demanding to know the long term value of the International Worship Centre project embarked upon by the Governor of Akwa Ibom State, Emmanuel Udom.

“In addition to other key infrastructure and industries we are building, we as a people are raising an altar for our God, even for generations yet unborn.”

Facebook user, Bassey Inyang said, “This project is complete waste of public funds that should have been channelled to the education and health sectors of the state. ]]> By Assam Abia.

The Paramount Ruler of Eket, His Majesty, Edidem E. C. D. Abia has shown great displeasure at the rampant cases of rape in Eket and has charged Village Heads to be at the forefront of fighting against the menace.

The Monarch made his displeasure known in a meeting held between all Village Heads and Village Council Chairmen in Eket over the weekend at his Palace.

Edidem Abia warned that no Village Head should attempt to settle rape cases in the community because like murder, rape is criminal in nature and should be reported to the police while victim is taken to the hospital for medical examination by a doctor and medical report produced.

The meeting between the Paramount Ruler and Village Heads came on the heels of a rape case reported in Ikot Odiong Eket where an innocent girl was gang raped by four boys in a bush. Some persons, according to report, attempted to settle the matter in the Village.

Edidem E. C. D. Abia warned that anybody or Village Head who tries to cover rape case is culpable of abating crime and must be sanctioned, adding that it was an offence to have canal knowledge with an under age girl or adult without her consent.

He called on parents to be alive to their responsibilities of bringing up their children in the fear of God and good morals, adding that any person caught in the act of rape does not deserve to live in the community.

The post Gov Wike Visits Akwa Ibom, Mourns Nkanga, PDP Chairman, Others appeared first on Naijablog.

]]> Governor Nyesom Wike of Rivers State has described the sudden dead of the Director General of Governor Udom Emmanuel’s two terms campaign organization and Chairman of Pan Niger Delta Forum, PANDEF, Air Commodore Idongesit Nkanga rtd and the
Akwa Ibom State Chairman of the Peoples Democratic Party, PDP, Mr. Udo Ekpenyong, as grave losses not just to Akwa Ibom State but the entire South-South region and the party which, he said, is Nigeria’s hope.

The Rivers State Governor stated this during a condolence visit to Governor Emmanuel at Hilltop Mansion, Government House Uyo, on Thursday.

“I came here because Governor Udom has lost two, three prominent sons of Akwa Ibom State, the most painful is the former two time Director-General of his campaign organisation and the Chairman of PANDEF and then his party Chairman which is also my party and the hope for this country, PDP.

“I said no what affects him, affects me, what touches Akwa Ibom touches Rivers state”, Wike said.

He said that Idongesit Nkanga’s death was unfortunate, untimely and a sad event for both the people of Akwa Ibom and Rivers states, explaining that the loss is happening in a time when he had become a strong voice in the Niger-Delta and been at the frontline of agitations for the restructuring of Nigeria.

He however encouraged Governor Emmanuel to draw consolation from the word of God and hold onto his faith as a Christian.

Governor Wike said that God is all-knowing and cannot be questioned by man over what he has allowed, while assuring the government of Rivers State will be fully involved in all levels of the funeral arrangements for the two.

Wike said Governor Emmanuel has earned his respect and the love of his people by his integrity in keeping his promises to his people and his political associates.

He described Governor Emmanuel’s developmental strides as impressive and the governor, as a man who loves his people.

“Governor Udom Emmanuel is my brother, he is a man of his words, if he tells you that he will do something you can go home and sleep with your two eyes closed, again Governor Udom Emmanuel has the interest of the people of the Niger Delta at heart particularly the people of Akwa Ibom State.

“As we were coming in today I told the Chief of Staff that Akwa Ibom is competing with Rivers State with what I have seen, it is unbelievable”.

Governor Emmanuel, in his response, thanked his Rivers State counterpart and other state governors in the Niger Delta region for identifying with him in the period of grief, describing his relationship with Wike as what he values beyond their tenures in office.

Governor Emmanuel described the departed two-time DG of his governorship campaign organsation, Idongesit Nkanga and the State PDP Chairman as men of proven integrity and loyalty, adding that the news of Nkanga’s death, in the peak of the yuletide, threw the state into an unimaginable grief.

In his words, “For a man to be your two terms campaign DG, he must have proven a high level of loyalty and integrity.

“If Idongesit Nkanga says he loves you in front, know that he loves you all round, both in front and behind you. He was a man of that kind of integrity, character and personality. You could see, even in the South-south, he never had any wall of discrimination”.

“My party Chairman also, in fact when they are introducing him, they call him Mr. Loyalty. He was that loyal.

“He has served the party both at the state and the national levels. In short, if you want any definition for loyal, just look for Hon. Udo Ekpenyong, you’ll certainly get that anytime any day. It’s so painful”, Emmanuel said.

The Akwa Ibom Governor also bemoaned that the state, within the period has lost a former Chief of Air Staff, Air Marshal Nsikak Eduok and former Commissioner for lands in the state Executive Council, Architect Ime Ekpo, adding that the state is pained that four of her very good people are to be buried within the same period.

He however accepted to be consoled with the fact that every man has a set time by God to quit the earth, despite the pain, and restated his resolve to continue to provide leadership for the people of the state, with the hope that God will give the state beauty for ashes.

Governor Emmanuel called on all governors, leaders and stakeholders of the Niger Delta region to join him and the people of Akwa Ibom State for the funeral ceremony in honour of the PANDEF Chairman, scheduled for February 6, 2021.
