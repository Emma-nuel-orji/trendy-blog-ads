Nachev, of West End Lane in Harlington, Hayes, was convicted of one count of murder and one count of wounding with intent.

Jurors were told the two young men were not friends but had a relationship “resulting from their involvement in the street selling of drugs in and around Hayes”.

]]> Former Pope, Benedict XVI is seriously ill with shingles according to a report in the Monday, August 3, 2020 edition of the German Passauer Neue Presse newspaper.

Shingles is a viral infection that causes a painful rash.

According to the report the Pope, now 93, is very frail and his voice is barely audible as the infection is causing episodes of severe pain

He became seriously ill after returning to the Vatican from a visit to Germany in June where he went to see his dying brother.

The pope is suffering from a virus that causes a facial rash and episodes of severe pain, the newspaper reported, citing the former pope’s biographer Peter Seewald.

According to Peter Seewald, the former pope’s biographer:

“the Pope emeritus is now extremely frail,” the report says. “His thinking and his memory are quick, but his voice is hardly audible at the moment.”

Seewald visited Benedict in Rome on Saturday August 1, 2020 to present him with his biography.

“At the meeting the emeritus Pope, despite his illness, was optimistic and declared that if his strength increased again he would possibly take up his pen again,” the paper said.

Georg Ratzinger, brother to Benedict, and aged 96, died shortly after he was visited by the former pope. It was Benedict’s first trip outside Italy since 2013, the year he resigned the papacy, citing a “lack of strength of mind and body” connected to his old age.

Pope Benedict, whose original name is Joseph Ratzinger, now lives in a small former monastery inside the Vatican and is being cared for daily by a team of doctors.

The post UAE Becomes First Arab Country To Produce Nuclear Energy After Successful Launch Of Newly Constructed Power Plant appeared first on Naijablog.

]]> UAE’s first nuclear reactor at the Barakah Nuclear Energy Plant has successfully started operations.

The United Arab Emirates has become the first Arabian country to produce nuclear energy, following the start-up of a newly constructed nuclear power plant in Abu Dhabi.

The Barakah Nuclear Power Plant’s Unit 1 is now using nuclear fuel to produce energy after the facility successfully executed initial tests on Saturday.

Three other reactors are still under construction and the plant still needs to be connected to the country’s power grid, local media reported.

“Today we announce the UAE’s success in operating the first peaceful nuclear reactor in the Arab world,” Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid, ruler of Dubai, wrote on Twitter.

He added that “the goal is to operate four nuclear power plants that will provide one-quarter of the country’s energy needs in a safe, reliable and emission-free way.”

Hamad Alkaabi, the UAE’s representative to the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), described the plant’s initial launch as a “historic milestone” for his country.

The Dubai leader also said the plant’s launch illustrates that “the Arabs are able to resume their scientific ambitions” and hinted that the next step for the UAE is space exploration.
