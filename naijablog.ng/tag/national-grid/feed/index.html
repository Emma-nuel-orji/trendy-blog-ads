The post Blackout across Nigeria as national grid collapses for the fifth time in 2024 appeared first on Naijablog.

]]> The national grid reportedly experienced a fresh collapse in the early hours of Monday, April 15, throwing the country into total darkness.

This comes barely three weeks after the grid collapsed on Thursday, March 28, the fourth in 2024.

BusinessDay reports that data obtained from the Nigerian System Operator’s portal, reveals the grid recorded an unprecedented zero Megawatts (MW) at t about 2:00 am and is currently generating a meagre 52.3MW around 7:10 am the same day.

According to the report, the consequences of this blackout were felt nationwide, as electricity distribution companies (DisCos) struggled to cope with the sudden and prolonged outage.

This collapse marks the fifth grid disturbance in 2024, adding to challenges that have long plagued Nigeria’s power sector.

]]> Nigerians have not had this before as it is now in term of Electricity supply in the country.

Despite huge amount of Many spend and continue been spend to give Nigerians steady power supply, almost every corner of the country is left with total darkness.

Just within the space of January to June 2022, Nigerian National grid has collapsed 17th times.

Despite all the collapsed and no supply of Electricity to Nigerian, the distribution company still go around collecting estimated bills from Nigerians.

In an exclusive interview with resident of Igo Community, Ekehuan road Benin city, Naijablog gathered that a two bedroom flat occupant paid ₦1500 each every month for light they don’t see, while a room and a Palo occupant paid ₦1000 each every month.
